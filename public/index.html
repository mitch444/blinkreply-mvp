DealerSocket Questions

Blink Response Engine sits upstream. DealerSocket stays the system of record.

Lead Intake Reality

  • Do all of your leads arrive through the same dsmessage ADF inbox?
  • Can you tell which leads were seen immediately versus buried in email noise?
  • When multiple leads arrive at once, who actually notices first?
  • Do you know how long a lead sat untouched before DealerSocket even logged activity?
  • Are reps reacting to alerts or stumbling onto leads later?
  • Normalizes every source into one event stream regardless of ADF or email noise.
  • Creates an immediate seen moment with a shared clock for all reps.
  • Logs the exact arrival time and first human action outside the CRM.
  • Prevents silent lead burial by pushing the moment to the team, not an inbox.
  • Preserves source and requestdate so DealerSocket receives clean context.

Response Time Illusion

  • Does DealerSocket show when a human actually responded or just when a status changed?
  • Can you see who saw the lead first but didn’t act?
  • Do you know how many leads were claimed late but reported as on time?
  • Can you tell the difference between speed and compliance?
  • Are your fastest reps rewarded or are slow reps protected?
  • Measures time to first human response, not time to status change.
  • Captures who saw it versus who acted for coaching clarity.
  • Produces a verifiable claim timestamp and response timestamp.
  • Makes compliance visible and speed visible in seconds.
  • Sends DealerSocket a lead that already has a clear owner and timeline.

Ownership and Behavior

  • Who really earns the lead, first response or first click?
  • Can two reps respond at the same time without clarity?
  • What happens when everyone assumes someone else is on it?
  • Do reps feel urgency or entitlement?
  • Is ownership clear in the first 30 seconds?
  • Ownership is awarded by first response, not first click.
  • One lead, one winner. Everyone else sees closed instantly.
  • Removes ambiguity during bursts of inbound leads.
  • Rewards speed and execution consistently, reducing entitlement.
  • Creates a fair contest loop that drives repeatable habits.

Manager Visibility Gap

  • Can you see response races happening in real time?
  • Do you know which leads triggered urgency and which didn’t?
  • Can you intervene while a lead is still alive?
  • Do you see missed opportunities or only bad outcomes later?
  • Are you coaching behavior or just reading reports after the fact?
  • Shows live contests: unclaimed, claimed, escalated, duplicate risk.
  • Escalates exceptions before the guest is gone using time thresholds.
  • Surfaces patterns by shift: hesitators, missed contests, slow periods.
  • Gives managers a coaching window while the lead is still alive.
  • Keeps DealerSocket as the record while Blink exposes the first 30 seconds.
