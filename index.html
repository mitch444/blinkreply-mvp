DealerSocket Questions

BlinkReply sits upstream of DealerSocket to enforce speed, clarity, and accountability without changing your CRM.

What problem does BlinkReply solve inside DealerSocket?

BlinkReply fixes the first 10 minutes. It creates instant visibility, speed, and clean ownership so leads don’t die in silence and handoffs are accountable.

How does the “contest” work?

First click wins. The first rep to click becomes the owner, and everyone sees the winner instantly. It’s simple, fair, and eliminates cherry-picking.

What stops a rep from claiming a lead and then doing nothing?

Ownership is conditional. If no meaningful activity happens within 15 minutes, Blink re-arms the lead and runs the contest again.

Meaningful activity includes:
  • Opening the lead record in DealerSocket (or via the Blink link)
  • Clicking “Next Steps / Outcomes” in Blink and selecting an outcome
  • Creating a note/task or logging a disposition/outcome
  • Sending a call/text/video through Blink tools (or logging the event)
  • Setting an appointment

If none are detected: ownership is revoked or the lead is re-contested, with the previous winner marked as “stalled” (visible, not shamed).

How do you detect meaningful activity if DealerSocket doesn’t expose every signal?

We implement layered enforcement. Exact event mapping depends on DealerSocket capabilities, but the enforcement loop is non-negotiable.

  • Best: DealerSocket events/webhooks (open, note, task, status, appointment)
  • Good: Blink-side actions (outcome click, video sent, call/text started)
  • Fallback: manager-confirmed outcome + timer-based re-contest
Does Blink replace DealerSocket or fight it?

No. Blink enforces response discipline and handoff clarity while DealerSocket remains the system of record.

Who sees what?
  • Reps: new lead alert + winner confirmation in real time
  • Managers: response timelines, stalled wins, and outcomes
What does the winner automatically get?

Clean handoff, zero ambiguity. The winner gets ownership assignment, a next-steps prompt, and optional video/call/text flows.

What happens to the “late” reps?

Visibility creates coaching, not punishment. Late counts inform training and staffing decisions without shaming individuals.

How do we prevent chaos or double-contacting the customer?

One owner at a time. There’s a single live status and audit trail. If re-contested, the lead is clearly marked and tracked.

What does implementation look like (realistically)?
  • MVP: lead forwarding → contest link → Blink outcomes → DealerSocket owner assignment + notes/tasks
  • Phase 2: deeper event signals/webhooks + richer automation

We’ll ship the enforcement loop before adding complexity.
